About the Journal

The DIAB Research Journal is a student- and faculty-led initiative at Dubai International Academy Al Barsha. Our mission is to provide a professional platform for publishing high-quality research in STEM, AI, Medicine, Social Sciences, and Aerospace.

Scope

  • Aerospace engineering (aerodynamics, avionics, propulsion, flight management systems)
  • Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning
  • STEM innovations, sustainability, and interdisciplinary projects
  • Medicine and social sciences

Dual Submission Policy

The DIAB Research Journal operates under a dual submission policy. Authors are free to submit their work here while also submitting to other journals or conferences. Our priority is to help young researchers gain visibility and improve their work.

Resources for Researchers

If you need help with your research work please contact the Editor-in-Chief. We can help you get in contact with the R&D section of our journal. Please remember that the purpose of the journal is to support early researchers, so please reach out for any form of support.

Submission Guidelines

The Dubai International Academy Al Barsha Research Journal is committed to maintaining high academic standards and providing a professional, student-driven platform for early researchers. Authors must carefully follow the guidelines below to ensure that their submission can be considered for peer review and publication.

1. Types of Accepted Submissions

  • Original Research Articles: Present original findings in STEM, AI, Medicine, Social Sciences or Aerospace. Minimum word count: 500 words, though most articles are 1000–6000 words.
  • Review Articles: Provide a critical overview of existing work. Minimum word count: 800 words, typically 3000–8000 words.
  • Lab or experiment reports, proposals and Case Studies: Summaries of projects, experiments, or engineering solutions relevant to our scope.

2. Manuscript Structure

We recommend the following structure:

  1. Title Page: Title, author names, affiliations, and contact email.
  2. Abstract: 150–250 words summarizing the work.
  3. Keywords: 3–6 words or phrases.
  4. Introduction: State the problem and context.
  5. Methods: Explain research methodology or approach.
  6. Results: Present findings clearly (figures and tables encouraged).
  7. Discussion: Interpret results and compare with prior work.
  8. Conclusion: Summarize contributions and suggest future work.
  9. References: APA style consistently applied.
  10. In-text citations: APA format using the "(name, year)" format.

3. Formatting Requirements

  • Manuscripts must be in English.
  • PDF submission only (upload via our form).
  • Figures and tables must be clearly labeled and referenced in text.
  • Equations should be typeset using proper math editors (not screenshots).

4. Ethical Considerations

  • Submissions must be the authors’ original work — plagiarism is strictly prohibited.
  • All co-authors must approve the final version before submission.
  • Data integrity must be maintained; falsification or fabrication is grounds for rejection.
  • Human or animal studies must comply with relevant ethical standards.

5. Peer Review and Revisions

  • All articles undergo blind peer review.
  • Authors may receive reviewer comments requesting minor or major revisions.
  • Revised manuscripts should be re-submitted with a point-by-point response.
  • Decisions: Accepted, Minor Revision, Major Revision, or Rejected.

6. Dual Submission Policy

To empower students, we allow dual submissions. Authors can submit their article to this journal while also submitting elsewhere. We only request that authors inform us if their work is accepted by another venue, so we can avoid duplicate publication.

7. Final Checklist

  • ✓ Title and abstract are complete and polished.
  • ✓ Word count ≥ 500 (research) or ≥ 1000 (review).
  • ✓ References formatted consistently.
  • ✓ Figures/tables are included with captions.
  • ✓ PDF uploaded via the submission form.

Journal Policies

The Dubai International Academy Al Barsha Research Journal (DIAB RJ) aims to give students a professional and supportive platform to publish high-quality research. To maintain fairness, academic honesty, and consistency, the following policies apply to all submissions.

1. Originality and Academic Integrity

All work submitted must be the author’s own. Plagiarism, copying from websites or AI tools without proper rewriting, or submitting work done by others is not allowed. Ideas, figures, text, and data taken from outside sources must be acknowledged using APA referencing. We reserve the right to reject or remove any work that violates academic honesty.

2. Use of AI Tools

AI tools may be used for language improvement, explanations, or organization, but AI must not create results, data, or full paragraphs that the author cannot personally justify. Authors are fully responsible for checking the accuracy of their work.

3. Authorship

All listed authors must have genuinely contributed to:

  • the idea or design of the project,
  • data collection or analysis,
  • writing and revising the manuscript.

All authors must approve the final version before submission.

4. Dual Submission Policy

To support young researchers, DIAB RJ allows dual submission. This means authors may submit their paper here while also sending it to another journal, competition, or conference.

We only ask that authors:

  • inform us if the work is accepted elsewhere,
  • ensure that the other venue also allows dual submissions,
  • avoid publishing the same final version twice without transparency.

Our goal is to help students build strong portfolios without limiting their opportunities.

5. Ethics and Data Accuracy

All results, figures, and conclusions must be honest and accurately reported. Fabricating or changing data is not acceptable. Projects involving surveys, interviews, or feedback from people must be conducted respectfully and with clear permission.

6. Review and Editing Process

All submissions are reviewed by student editors and/or faculty reviewers. Feedback may request small or major improvements. Authors are expected to revise their work accordingly and resubmit.

Final decisions include:

  • Accept
  • Minor Revision
  • Major Revision
  • Reject

Once accepted, the journal may make light formatting or grammar adjustments before publication.

7. Corrections After Publication

If a significant mistake is found after publication, authors may request a correction. If the journal identifies an error affecting accuracy or ethics, we may update or remove the article with an explanation.

8. Purpose of These Policies

These guidelines exist to protect student authors, maintain academic integrity, and ensure that all published work reflects honest effort and respectful research practices.
